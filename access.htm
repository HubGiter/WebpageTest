ACCESS

  1. Instant Messaging: Cellular communications have allowed the world to setup networks around the Earth that let people communicate almost instantly. This has allowed people hundreds of kilometers away to communicate with each other to schedule plans, send an important message, or just talk about something.
  2. Video Sharing Websites: Websites like YouTube have allowed people to create content and put it out for the world to see. People can communicate and explain ideas with helpful videos, have their opinion be listened to and spread, or create entertaining content that anyone around the world with the right technology can see.
  3. Q&A Websites: Question and answer websites allow users to ask a question and get help from another user. There are more general websites like “Yahoo! Answers” that allow anything to be asked and it’s sorted into different categories that people search through until someone wiser aids the person in need. To very specific websites like Stack Overflow, which brings together computer programmers who ask and answer questions as they all try to improve and meet their goals.
  4. Research: The internet has allowed for people to share information online to be accessible to almost anyone. If someone needs to learn about a topic they can use browsers that will find many sources of information. From news articles, to published research documents, to even their government's official website.
  5. Social Media: Sites and apps like Instagram, Snapchat, Twitter, and Myspace allow people to express what they like, discover more about what they love, and stay informed on what’s happening in the world around them. Social media websites allow people to share their ideas almost instantly and update the world on what’s happening with them. As well as follow what they like and what is trending so that they can stay updated whenever something that is new and interesting to them happens.
